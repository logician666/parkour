# Phase 2 — Static Strength `Weeks 3–4`

> **Goal:** Build foundational strength in positions required for parkour — isometric endurance, push strength, wall support.
> **Unlock criteria:** Phase 1 complete. Can hold deep squat 60s and plank 40s.

---

## Session 1 — Push & Hold

**Warm-up:** 5 min — wrist circles, bear crawl, arm swings

---

### Incline Push-Up (hands on wall)
**Sets · Reps · Rest:** 4 × 10–15 reps · 60s rest
**Log type:** Reps

> **Why:** Builds pushing strength with lower injury risk. Prepares chest, triceps, and front shoulders for vault arm placements.

**How:** Stand facing wall, about arm's length away. Hands on wall at chest height, slightly wider than shoulders. Lean forward, body straight. Bend elbows to bring chest toward wall, then push back. To increase difficulty, step further from wall.

**Cues:**
- Body straight as a plank — no hip sag
- Elbows flare at ~45°, not fully out
- Chest touches wall, not just nose

[Video demonstration](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cfns5WseW5o)

---

### Wall Sit
**Sets · Reps · Rest:** 4 × 30–60s hold · 60s rest
**Log type:** Time (seconds)

> **Why:** Builds quad endurance and isometric strength needed for crouched positions, balance on obstacles, and pre-jump setup holds.

**How:** Back against wall. Slide down until thighs are parallel to floor and knees at 90°. Feet flat, directly under knees. Arms at sides or crossed. Hold.

**Cues:**
- Thighs parallel to floor is the target
- Knees at 90° — not behind toes
- Do not rest hands on knees

[Video demonstration](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=y-wV4Venusw)

---

### Hollow Body Hold
**Sets · Reps · Rest:** 3 × 20–40s hold · 60s rest
**Log type:** Time (seconds)

> **Why:** The hollow body is the gymnastic/parkour foundation for all body tension movements — vaults, tic-tacs, wall runs require this rigidity.

**How:** Lie on back, arms extended overhead on floor. Press lower back firmly into floor. Lift shoulders (~10cm) and legs (~15–30cm) simultaneously. Body forms a slight bowl shape. If too hard, bend knees or lower arms to sides.

**Cues:**
- Lower back MUST be pressed into floor — non-negotiable
- If back lifts off — raise legs higher
- Breathe — short exhales

[Video demonstration](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LlDNef_Ztsc)

---

### Wall-Supported Handstand Hold
**Sets · Reps · Rest:** 3 × 10–30s hold · 90s rest
**Log type:** Time (seconds)

> **Why:** Shoulder pressing strength and proprioception for vault arm placements and wall runs. Builds wrist, shoulder, and midline stability.

**How:** Stand ~30cm from wall, facing it. Place hands on floor ~30cm from wall, shoulder-width. Kick one leg up, then the other. Body forms an inverted line against wall. Push the floor away — active straight arms. Come down by stepping one foot at a time — never collapse.

**Cues:**
- Arms fully straight — elbows locked
- Push floor away — active shoulders
- Come down controlled

[Video demonstration](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fPo_HfPiOmo)

**Cool-down:** 5 min — doorway chest stretch, wrist flexor stretch, shoulder cross-body stretch

## Session 2 — Posterior Chain & Grip

**Warm-up:** 5 min — ankle circles, hip hinges, arm swings

---

### Single-Leg Glute Bridge
**Sets · Reps · Rest:** 3 × 12 each side · 45s rest
**Log type:** Reps

> **Why:** Single-leg strength is the real-world demand of parkour — every landing and push-off is fundamentally single-leg. Unilateral training exposes and corrects asymmetries.

**How:** Lie on back, knees bent, feet flat. Extend one leg straight in air. Drive heel of planted foot into floor, squeeze glute, raise hips until body is straight. Hold 1 second. Lower over 3 seconds. Complete all reps on one side then switch.

**Cues:**
- Hips level — do not rotate toward raised leg
- Squeeze glute of working side
- If weak side drops hips — reduce reps until equal

[Video demonstration](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LM8XfnEPuKg)

---

### Grip Strengthener — Slow Squeeze
**Sets · Reps · Rest:** 3 × 10 reps each hand (3s squeeze) · 45s rest
**Log type:** Reps

> **Why:** Grip is load-bearing in parkour — catching obstacles, ledge hangs, and scaffolding require sustained grip strength under body weight.

**How:** Hold grip strengthener in one hand. Slowly close against resistance over 2 seconds. Hold closed for 3 seconds (maximum squeeze). Open slowly over 2 seconds. That is 1 rep. Weaker hand first.

**Cues:**
- 3 second hold is the stimulus — don't rush
- Full open between reps
- Use whole hand — not fingers only

[Video demonstration](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fKn_2Nj1AoE)

---

### Reverse Nordic Curl (assisted)
**Sets · Reps · Rest:** 3 × 8–10 reps · 60s rest
**Log type:** Reps

> **Why:** Hamstring and glute strength in hip extension position — the primary driver of explosive jumps. One of the highest-yield exercises for injury prevention in running and jumping sports.

**How:** Kneel on floor. Hook feet under a heavy object (bottom of door, heavy bag). From upright kneeling, lower your torso FORWARD and DOWN by bending at knees, controlling with hamstrings. Use hands to catch yourself at the bottom. Push back up with hands. Over weeks, use hands less.

**Cues:**
- Body straight from knee to head — no hip hinge
- Hamstrings control the descent — feel the burn in back of thigh
- Lower as far as you can control without crashing

[Video demonstration](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=W3k0HE8QZGE)

---

### Finger Band — Isolated Finger Extensions
**Sets · Reps · Rest:** 3 × 10 each finger · 30s rest
**Log type:** Reps

> **Why:** Individual finger extensor conditioning prevents pulley injuries — the most common training injury among traceurs and climbers.

**How:** Place band around all 5 fingers. Extend ONE finger at a time against the band — index, middle, ring, pinky, thumb. 10 extensions per finger per hand.

**Cues:**
- Slow deliberate movement per finger
- You will feel fatigue in forearm — correct
- If tendons ache, stop immediately

[Video demonstration](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fYBCn7uJKQA)

**Cool-down:** 5 min — seated hamstring stretch, kneeling hip flexor stretch, child's pose

## Session 3 — Full Body Integration

**Warm-up:** 5 min — bear crawl, lateral lunges, arm circles

---

### Push-Up
**Sets · Reps · Rest:** 4 × max reps (stop 2 before failure) · 90s rest
**Log type:** Reps (log your max set)

> **Why:** Full horizontal push strength benchmark. Also tests core stiffness under dynamic load.

**How:** High plank — hands slightly wider than shoulders, body straight head-to-heel. Lower chest to floor, elbows at ~45° to body. When chest nearly touches floor, push up explosively. Full extension at top. If too hard, knees on floor keeping hips level.

**Cues:**
- Chest, hips, and knees reach floor simultaneously — not nose first
- Look down 30cm ahead — neutral neck
- Log your max reps — this is your strength progression marker

[Video demonstration](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_l3ySVKYVJ8)

---

### Squat Jump (low intensity)
**Sets · Reps · Rest:** 4 × 8 reps · 90s rest
**Log type:** Reps

> **Why:** Introduces plyometric demand safely. The squat jump is the simplest form of the take-off and landing cycle central to parkour.

**How:** Feet shoulder-width. Descend into squat until thighs parallel. Drive through heels and jump as high as possible. Land softly — toes first, then heels, bending knees immediately to absorb force (land into a quarter squat). Never land stiff-legged.

**Cues:**
- SOFT landing — the landing teaches your nervous system
- Arms swing up during jump for height
- Control descent — do not crash

[Video demonstration](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CVaEhXotL7M)

---

### Side Plank
**Sets · Reps · Rest:** 3 each side × 20–40s hold · 30s between sides
**Log type:** Time (seconds)

> **Why:** Lateral core stability prevents the spine from collapsing sideways during directional changes and one-sided landings.

**How:** Lie on side. Prop on one forearm, elbow under shoulder. Stack feet or stagger for balance. Lift hips until body is straight head-to-feet. Hold. Repeat on other side.

**Cues:**
- Hips up — do not sag
- Top arm on hip or extend upward for challenge
- Both sides equal time — note any asymmetry

[Video demonstration](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=K2iEpUPiMoY)

**Cool-down:** 5 min — spinal twist, pigeon pose, doorway chest open
