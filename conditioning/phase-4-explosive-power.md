# Phase 4 — Explosive Power `Weeks 7–8`

> **Goal:** Train the nervous system for rapid force production — the physical hallmark of a traceur.
> **Unlock criteria:** Phase 3 complete. Nordic curl 80% depth. Stick landing quality 4/5. Dead hang 40s.
> **Critical rule:** Every plyometric set requires full recovery. Do NOT shorten rest periods. Power output, not endurance, is the training stimulus. Fatigue kills the adaptation.

## Session 1 — Lower Explosive

**Warm-up:** 8 min — hip circles, leg swings, bodyweight squats ×15, lateral lunges ×10 each, 3 sub-maximal squat jumps

---

### Maximum Jump to Stick
**Sets · Reps · Rest:** 5 × 4 reps · 2 min rest
**Log type:** Quality (1–5 — rate landing control and silence)

> **Why:** The reactive landing stimulus comes from the magnitude of force at impact. Without a raised surface, that force is generated by jumping as high as possible — what goes up must come down. A maximal jump produces peak ground reaction force on landing, training the nervous system to decelerate rapidly under real load.

**How:** Jump as high as possible — full effort, arms driving overhead. At peak height, pull into a slight tuck. On landing: toes first, absorb immediately into a deep squat, completely still. The landing must be silent. Hold the landing position for 3 full seconds before the next rep. The silence and stillness are the quality metric.

**Cues:**
- Maximum jump height — this is not a casual squat jump. Full effort.
- Tuck slightly at peak — prepares the body for the absorption
- Land toes-first, absorb through ankle, knee, hip in sequence — like a spring compressing
- Silent landing = quality 5. Loud thud = 1–2. Stumble = 1.
- The 3-second hold is mandatory — it confirms control, not just survival of the landing

![](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=34yBjkF5gkg)

---

### Broad Jump
**Sets · Reps · Rest:** 5 × 4 reps · 2 min rest
**Log type:** Distance (cm or floor tiles)

> **Why:** Horizontal power is the engine of parkour — crossing gaps, launching into vaults, covering ground rapidly. The broad jump is its purest measure and training vehicle.

**How:** Stand with feet shoulder-width. Drop into a quarter squat, swing arms back. Explode forward — drive arms forward and up as you extend hips and knees simultaneously. Reach for maximum horizontal distance. Land in a controlled quarter squat, stick the landing. Mark your distance consistently (e.g., tape on floor).

**Cues:**
- The arm swing generates significant power — use it fully
- Drive hips, not just knees
- Reach forward with your feet at landing
- Stick every landing — the deceleration is part of the exercise
- Log distance each set — track your best

![](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XqpN9AbLMe4)

---

### Single-Leg Bound to Stick
**Sets · Reps · Rest:** 4 × 5 each leg · 2 min rest
**Log type:** Quality (1–5)

> **Why:** Every precision jump, every vault landing is single-leg in practice. Bilateral power is necessary but insufficient. This trains explosive single-leg push-off and the unilateral landing control that bilateral training cannot develop.

**How:** Stand on one leg. Drive that knee up as you push off, leaping forward onto the same foot (same-leg bound) or onto the other foot (alternating). Land on one foot and hold completely still for 3 seconds. Log quality of the held landing.

**Cues:**
- Drive the free knee forward and up — it adds distance and controls landing
- Land on the ball of the foot, absorb through the ankle, knee, and hip
- The 3-second hold is mandatory — it measures real control
- Alternate legs across sets, not within a set

![](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=l3KNrID-QWo)

---

### Lateral Bound
**Sets · Reps · Rest:** 4 × 6 each side · 90s rest
**Log type:** Distance (cm or floor tiles)

> **Why:** Lateral explosive power for directional changes, gap traversals at angles, and the lateral launches common in urban environments. Most training is sagittal — this fills the frontal-plane gap.

**How:** Stand on one foot. Push explosively sideways (laterally), landing on the opposite foot. Stick the landing. Push back. That is 1 rep each side. Reach for maximum width.

**Cues:**
- Push from the hip — not just a sideways step
- Absorb on the landing foot — single leg, controlled
- Keep the torso upright — resist lateral lean
- Track distance consistently

![](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rzSdiA1WoW4)

**Cool-down:** 8 min — pigeon pose extended hold (90s each), standing quad stretch, calf stretches, full hip flexor series

## Session 2 — Upper Explosive

**Warm-up:** 5 min — wrist circles, passive hang 20s, incline push-ups ×10, arm swings

---

### Explosive Push-Up (hands leave floor)
**Sets · Reps · Rest:** 5 × 4 reps · 2 min rest
**Log type:** Quality (1–5 — hands fully leaving floor = 5)

> **Why:** The push phase of a vault is explosive, not slow. When you place hands on an obstacle and push your body over, the push must be fast and powerful. This trains exactly that — explosive elbow extension under body weight.

**How:** Start in push-up position. Lower to the floor with control. Then push explosively — with enough force that your hands leave the floor at the top. Land with hands back in position and absorb the return. Begin with knees on floor if needed. Progress to full push-up position.

**Cues:**
- The eccentric (lowering) is controlled — the concentric (push) is maximal
- Push through the entire hand — don't just push from fingertips
- Keep core rigid throughout — no hip pike on the push
- Rate 5 = hands clearly airborne. Rate 3 = almost leaves floor. Rate 1 = fast but grounded.

![](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Pd3VxHIopZE)

---

### Plyometric Pike Push-Up
**Sets · Reps · Rest:** 4 × 4 reps · 2 min rest
**Log type:** Quality (1–5)

> **Why:** Explosive vertical pushing — the pattern required when vaulting over high obstacles where the arms must explosively straighten to lift the body's centre of mass above the obstacle.

**How:** Start in pike position (inverted V). Lower your head toward the floor between your hands, then push explosively upward. The goal is to have your hands briefly leave the floor. Begin with a faster-than-normal push if full airtime isn't yet achievable.

**Cues:**
- Keep hips high — the movement is purely at the shoulder
- Push through the full hand, not just palm heel
- Land with soft elbows — do not lock elbows on landing impact
- Progress: fast push → partial airtime → full airtime

![](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5wsTCNweCpQ)

---

### Wall Push (explosive isometric)
**Sets · Reps · Rest:** 4 × 6 reps · 60s rest
**Log type:** Quality (1–5)

> **Why:** Maximal neural activation for the pushing muscles without the injury risk of ballistic loading. The explosive intent against an immovable object recruits near-maximum motor units — training the nervous system to fire fast even when the load is heavier than bodyweight.

**How:** Stand 30cm from wall, hands on wall at chest height. In a controlled push-up position against the wall. Explosively push into the wall with 100% effort for 1 second. Fully relax for 2 seconds. That is 1 rep. This is intent-based training — the wall doesn't move but your nervous system responds to the intent.

**Cues:**
- 100% effort for exactly 1 second — then completely relax
- Brace the entire body during the push — not just the arms
- Breathe: inhale before, exhale forcefully during push
- This should feel maximally effortful, not just hard

---

### Hanging Scapular Pull + Explosive Pull Initiation
**Sets · Reps · Rest:** 4 × 5 reps · 90s rest
**Log type:** Reps

> **Why:** The scapular pull is the initiation of every climbing movement — the moment before a pull-up where the lats engage and the shoulder depresses. Training this movement explosively develops the reactivity needed for fast obstacle transitions.

**How:** Hang from door frame, arms fully extended. Without bending elbows, depress your scapulae explosively — shoulders pull sharply down and body rises 3–5cm. Hold 1 second at the top. Slowly return to dead hang. That is 1 rep. The explosive intent is key — not a slow squeeze.

**Cues:**
- Arms stay straight — this is a shoulder movement only
- Feel the lats engage at the initiation
- The rise is small but distinct
- If you cannot feel the difference between passive and active position, revisit [[phase-1-foundation|Phase 1's]] passive hang drill

![](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=V0hzdT1CZnk)

**Cool-down:** 8 min — doorway chest stretch, cross-body shoulder stretch, wrist decompression (shake out, then passive flexor stretch), thoracic rotation

## Session 3 — Reactive Circuit

**Warm-up:** 8 min — full joint circuit from [[phase-1-foundation|Phase 1 Session 1]] (all 4 exercises, 1 set each)

> **Circuit structure:** Complete all exercises back-to-back with 20s rest between exercises. Rest 3 min between full circuit rounds. Complete 4 rounds. The circuit trains the reactive quality — moving explosively under accumulating fatigue while maintaining landing precision.

---

### Round structure (4 rounds, 3 min between rounds)

#### Exercise A — Squat Jump to Stick
4 reps · 20s rest after

Same as [[phase-2-static-strength|Phase 2]], but now the standard is: stick every rep, hold 3 seconds, quality 4/5 minimum. If quality drops below 3, rest longer between rounds.

---

#### Exercise B — Explosive Push-Up
4 reps · 20s rest after

Same as Session 2. Maintain explosiveness even in round 4. If hands stop leaving the floor, the rest period was insufficient — increase to 4 min between rounds.

---

#### Exercise C — Lateral Bound (3 each side)
3 each side · 20s rest after

Same as Session 1. Focus on the stick landing on the receiving foot. Distance is secondary to control in the circuit context.

---

#### Exercise D — Broad Jump
3 reps · 3 min rest (full round recovery)

The final exercise of each round. Drive for maximum distance. This ends the round — take the full 3 min before repeating.

---

**Cool-down:** 10 min — extended hip flexor series, thoracic extension over chair back, pigeon pose, quad stretch, wrist and shoulder decompression
