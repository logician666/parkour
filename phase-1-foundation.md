# Phase 1 — Foundation `Weeks 1–2`

> **Goal:** Prepare joints, establish baseline mobility, activate deep stabilisers. No intensity — only precision.
> **Unlock for Phase 2:** Complete all 3 sessions in both weeks (6 sessions total).

[[_hub|← Hub]]

---

## Session 1 — Joint Prep & Crawl

**Warm-up:** 3 min gentle walking in place + 10 slow neck rolls each direction

---

### Wrist Circles & Flexor Stretch
**Sets · Reps · Rest:** 3 × 10 circles each direction + 20s hold · 30s rest
**Log type:** Quality (1–5)

> **Why:** Traceurs bear significant wrist load on vaults and precision landings. Pre-conditioning prevents injury.

**How:** Extend one arm forward, palm down. With other hand, gently pull fingers back for 20s. Then make a fist and rotate the wrist 10× clockwise, 10× counter-clockwise. Repeat other side.

**Cues:**
- Keep elbow straight during stretch
- Slow controlled circles — not fast
- Feel tension, not pain

[Video demonstration](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mSZWSQSSEjE)

---

### Ankle Circles & Calf Raises
**Sets · Reps · Rest:** 3 × 10 circles + 15 raises · 30s rest
**Log type:** Reps

> **Why:** Ankle stability is the #1 injury prevention factor in parkour. Calf raises build tendon resilience for landings.

**How:** Stand near wall for balance. Lift one foot, rotate ankle 10× each direction. Then stand on both feet, rise onto toes as high as possible, lower slowly (3 seconds down). That is 1 rep.

**Cues:**
- Full range of motion on circles
- Slow eccentric (lowering) is the training stimulus
- Keep heels off ground at top

[Video demonstration](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=g4C8gQR_4Jw)

---

### Bear Crawl
**Sets · Reps · Rest:** 4 × 10 steps forward + 10 back · 45s rest
**Log type:** Quality (1–5)

> **Why:** The foundational movement of parkour. Builds contralateral coordination, shoulder stability, hip mobility, and wrist conditioning simultaneously.

**How:** Start on all fours, knees hovering 2cm above floor. Move right hand + left foot forward simultaneously, then left hand + right foot. Keep back flat, hips low (same height as shoulders). Go forward then reverse to stay in your space.

**Cues:**
- Hips level — not bobbing
- Breathe steadily
- Look slightly forward, not down

[Video demonstration](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7xf3nAXFiJk)

---

### Finger Band Extensions
**Sets · Reps · Rest:** 3 × 15 reps · 30s rest
**Log type:** Reps

> **Why:** Extensors are chronically undertrained vs. gripping muscles, causing tendon imbalance — a primary cause of finger injuries in traceurs and climbers.

**How:** Place finger extension band around all 5 fingers. Spread all fingers outward against band resistance as wide as possible. Hold 1 second open, return slowly. Weaker hand first.

**Cues:**
- Both hands equal sets
- Slow return — eccentric matters
- No pain in tendons — discomfort is ok

[Video demonstration](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fYBCn7uJKQA)

**Cool-down:** 5 min — seated forward fold, pigeon pose each side, wrist prayer stretch

## Session 2 — Hip Mobility & Core Activation

**Warm-up:** 2 min walking + 10 hip circles each leg

---

### Deep Squat Hold
**Sets · Reps · Rest:** 3 × 30–60s hold · 45s rest
**Log type:** Time (seconds)

> **Why:** The deep squat is the resting parkour position — used in pre-jump setup, landing absorption, and ground movement. Must be comfortable and stable.

**How:** Feet shoulder-width, toes slightly out (15–30°). Lower slowly until thighs pass parallel — ideally thighs against calves. Arms out front for balance. Heels must stay flat. If heels rise, widen stance or place folded towel under heels temporarily.

**Cues:**
- Chest up — no rounding forward
- Push knees out in line with toes
- Work toward 60s over the 2 weeks

[Video demonstration](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HwnJe1nFMoI)

---

### Dead Bug
**Sets · Reps · Rest:** 3 × 8 reps each side · 45s rest
**Log type:** Reps

> **Why:** Trains deep core anti-extension — the ability to keep a rigid torso while limbs move. Critical for controlled vaults and landings.

**How:** Lie on back, arms pointing up, knees bent 90° in air. Slowly extend right arm back + left leg forward simultaneously, as far as possible without lower back rising. Return and switch. That is 1 rep.

**Cues:**
- Lower back must stay in contact with floor
- Exhale on extension
- Move slow — 3 seconds each way

[Video demonstration](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=g_BYB0R-4Ws)

---

### Glute Bridge
**Sets · Reps · Rest:** 3 × 15 reps · 30s rest
**Log type:** Reps

> **Why:** Activates glutes which are often inhibited from sitting. Glutes power jumps, vaults, and absorb landing forces through the hip chain.

**How:** Lie on back, knees bent, feet flat hip-width apart. Drive heels into floor and squeeze glutes to raise hips until body forms a straight line shoulder-to-knee. Hold 1 second at top. Lower slowly over 3 seconds.

**Cues:**
- Squeeze glutes — don't just push hips up
- Do not hyperextend lower back at top
- Feet flat, not on toes

[Video demonstration](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8bbE64NuDTU)

---

### Passive Hang — Door Frame Grip
**Sets · Reps · Rest:** 3 × 15–30s hold · 60s rest
**Log type:** Time (seconds)

> **Why:** Decompresses spine, builds grip endurance, conditions connective tissue of the shoulder complex — essential before any hanging or swinging movement.

**How:** Grip the top of your door frame with both hands, palms away. Step off the ground slightly or bend knees. Let body weight hang fully — do not pull up. Shoulders rise passively toward ears, then actively depress them (pull shoulders down away from ears). Hold in that active position.

**Cues:**
- Start with bent knees if door frame is low
- Active depressed shoulders — not passive shrug
- Grip central part of frame — do not stress frame joints

[Video demonstration](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=X8VUHIqUvz0)

**Cool-down:** 5 min — lying piriformis stretch, supine twist, doorway chest stretch

## Session 3 — Movement Patterns

**Warm-up:** 3 min bear crawl warm-up + 10 hip hinges

---

### Hip Hinge — Romanian Deadlift Pattern
**Sets · Reps · Rest:** 3 × 12 reps · 45s rest
**Log type:** Reps

> **Why:** The hip hinge is the movement foundation for all explosive parkour actions — jumps, take-offs, and absorbing landings via the posterior chain.

**How:** Feet hip-width. Hands on hips or extended forward. Push hips BACK (not down) while maintaining a flat back, hinging at the hip. Let chest drop toward floor, feeling a hamstring stretch. When you feel significant tension, drive hips forward to return upright. This is NOT a squat — knees stay relatively straight.

**Cues:**
- Back flat — imagine balancing a glass of water on your back
- Push hips back like opening a car door with your hip
- You should feel it in hamstrings, not lower back

[Video demonstration](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XxWcirHIwVo)

---

### Lateral Lunge
**Sets · Reps · Rest:** 3 × 10 each side · 45s rest
**Log type:** Reps

> **Why:** Parkour requires rapid lateral weight shifts — stepping out of runs, adjusting landing angles. Lateral lunge develops single-leg frontal plane control.

**How:** Stand feet together. Take a wide step directly to the right. Bend right knee deeply, keeping left leg straight. Right foot flat, toes forward. Push off right foot to return. Repeat on left. That is 1 rep per side.

**Cues:**
- Chest up — no forward rounding
- Knee tracks over foot — not caving inward
- Feel inner thigh stretch on straight leg

[Video demonstration](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gwWv7aPcD88)

---

### Plank
**Sets · Reps · Rest:** 3 × 20–45s hold · 45s rest
**Log type:** Time (seconds)

> **Why:** Core stiffness (not just strength) protects the spine and transfers force efficiently during landings and vaults. The plank builds this fundamental stiffness.

**How:** Lie face down. Forearms on floor, elbows under shoulders. Lift hips until body forms a straight line head-to-heel. Squeeze glutes, brace abs, squeeze quads. Breathe in short shallow breaths.

**Cues:**
- Glutes and abs simultaneously contracted
- Head neutral — look at floor 30cm ahead
- Push elbows into floor for lat activation

[Video demonstration](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pvIjsG5Svck)

---

### Quadruped Shoulder Taps
**Sets · Reps · Rest:** 3 × 10 each side · 45s rest
**Log type:** Reps

> **Why:** Develops anti-rotation core stability and shoulder proprioception — both required for controlled vault landings and arm placements.

**How:** Start in push-up position. Keeping hips perfectly level, lift right hand to tap left shoulder. Return. Lift left hand to tap right shoulder. That is 1 rep. Hips must NOT rotate — if they do, widen your feet.

**Cues:**
- Hips must stay square — zero rotation is the whole exercise
- Slow controlled lift
- Brace core before each tap

[Video demonstration](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AEfrVsF2EkY)

**Cool-down:** 5 min — child's pose, cat-cow ×10, standing hip flexor stretch
